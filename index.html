Global Temperature Trends Since 1960

Global warming - fake news or humanity's biggest threat?

This data visualisation will explore exactly how the global temperature has been changing over the past six decades through a series of maps and graphs. To explore this data, a baseline has been defined as the average temperature between 1951 to 1980. All comparative data will use this period as a baseline for comparison, thus all change refers to change from this period of time.

Please note all missing data has been replaced with 0, indicating no change since baseline.

Figure 1 is a choropleth map showing the average of the change in termperatures per month of each country between 1960 to 2018 compated to the baseline. This clearly shows that every country has become warmer on average over this time period. However, from this graph alone it is impossible to tell the trend since the data has been stripped of its temporal component during the calculations.

Figure 2 is a line chart of the average change in temperature compared to the baseline of every country by year. This temperature has not been normalized for land mass nor population but has excluded all missing data instead of replacing data with a 0. The trend over time becomes much more apparent with this graph as the temperature is clearly increasing over time.

Figure 3 shows a choropleth with a year filter scale to show the average temperatures of each country by year as you move through the slider. This can be used to give a visual representation of the temperature of each country by year and it can clearly be seen that almost every country becomes warmer over time, and the globe as a whole is becoming warmer.

Figure 4 shows a line chart of the number of countries that are below the baseline per year. In other words, this shows the number of countries that was actually cooler on average compared to the baseline period. Although fluctuation can be seen in the early periods of this data, eventually all countries become warmer than the baseline period. Interestingly in 2017 and 2018, not a single country was colder than the baseline period.
